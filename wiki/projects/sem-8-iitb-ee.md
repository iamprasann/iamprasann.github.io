# SEM-8-IITB-EE

`SEM-8-IITB-EE` is a final-semester repository with a tighter, more elective profile than the earlier semester archives. Instead of broad multi-course accumulation, it captures four advanced areas: biomedical microsystems, research and development, restructured power systems, and cryptology. The overall impression is of a semester centered on higher-level project work, research material, and specialized technical topics.

## Overview

- Repository: [iamprasann/SEM-8-IITB-EE](https://github.com/iamprasann/SEM-8-IITB-EE)
- Created: September 6, 2023
- Updated: September 6, 2023
- Primary language on GitHub: Jupyter Notebook

Top-level course folders:

- `BB610 - Biomedical Microsystems`
- `EE691 - Research and Development`
- `EE722 - Restructured Power Systems`
- `EE793 - Topics in Cryptology`

The root README is minimal, so the semester narrative comes mainly from the course names and preserved artifacts.

## Why It Mattered

This repository matters because it shows the undergraduate record at a later stage, when the work appears to shift away from routine course mechanics and toward specialized topics, project framing, and research-style deliverables. The folder mix suggests:

- topic-driven coursework supported by papers and planning documents
- research and implementation work around GPS spoofing
- collaborative and assignment-based work in power systems
- notebook-based cryptology work

That makes this repo useful not only as a semester archive but also as evidence of the thematic breadth at the end of the degree.

## How It Worked

Each course folder has a different mode of work.

- `BB610 - Biomedical Microsystems` looks like a project-based course supported by team/topic planning documents, deadlines, and research-paper PDFs.
- `EE691 - Research and Development` is centered on a GPS spoofing topic and preserves both reports and implementation artifacts. The `final-things` folder includes Python scripts, GNU Radio flowgraphs, screenshots, and data-like files, making it the most explicitly experimental folder in the repo.
- `EE722 - Restructured Power Systems` combines assignments with collaboration folders. The visible contents suggest a blend of individual submissions, group reports, presentations, and market-oriented artifacts such as yearly price and volume PDFs.
- `EE793 - Topics in Cryptology` preserves notebooks and assignment material, indicating a course where computational exploration and notebook-based project work were central.

The clearest first-pass theme is specialization. `SEM-8-IITB-EE` reads less like a semester log and more like a cluster of advanced electives with concrete technical outputs.
