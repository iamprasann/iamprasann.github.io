## Design Notes

### Top-level `ALU.vhd`

- The top-level entity exposes two select lines, 16-bit inputs `A` and `B` as individual bit ports, 16-bit outputs `R`, plus carry `C` and zero flag `Z`.
- `full_xor`
- `full_nand`
- `full_adder`
- `full_subtract`
- `full_zero`
- `MUX_4to1`

The design computes XOR, NAND, addition, and subtraction in parallel, then uses sixteen `MUX_4to1` instances to select the output result bit-by-bit based on `S1` and `S0`. A final mux selects the appropriate carry output, and `full_zero` derives the zero flag from the result lines.

This makes the project a structurally composed 16-bit ALU rather than a monolithic behavioral description.

### Supporting modules

- The repo contains lower-level helper modules such as `my_and.vhd`, `my_or.vhd`, `my_not.vhd`, `my_xor.vhd`, `my_nand.vhd`, `my_carry.vhd`, and `my_gen.vhd`.
- It also contains higher-level arithmetic/logic blocks such as `full_adder.vhd`, `full_subtract.vhd`, `full_xor.vhd`, `full_nand.vhd`, and `full_zero.vhd`.

This suggests a bottom-up design style built from primitive gates and reusable composed blocks.

### Testbench

- `Testbench.vhd` instantiates the ALU and applies many explicit bit-pattern cases across the four select-line modes.
- The comments describe it as a testbench with possible corner cases.

This indicates that verification was part of the project, not just design entry.
